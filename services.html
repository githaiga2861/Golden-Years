The Skilled Team Behind Your Care

Golden Years brings together a multidisciplinary team of licensed and trained professionals — so we can meet a wide range of clinical and personal needs under one trusted roof.

Licensed Practical Nurses (LPNs)

Service Details

A Closer Look at What We Offer

Personal Care & Assistance with ADLs

Personal care is the heart of home-based support. Our aides assist with the Activities of Daily Living — bathing, dressing, grooming, oral care, toileting and incontinence care, transfers, walking and mobility, and repositioning. We also help with the instrumental activities that keep a household running: meal preparation, light housekeeping, laundry, and transportation assistance to appointments and errands. Every personal-care plan is built and supervised by our nursing team, and delivered with patience, respect, and attention to each person's preferences and routines.

Medication Management & Assistance

Medication errors are one of the most common and preventable causes of hospitalization for older adults. Our team supports safe medication use through reminders, organization, and — where clinically appropriate and properly delegated — administration assistance. We coordinate with pharmacies and physicians, watch for side effects and interactions, and keep families informed, helping ensure the right medication is taken in the right dose at the right time.

Chronic Disease Management

Living with a long-term condition such as diabetes, heart disease, COPD, hypertension, or kidney disease requires consistent, knowledgeable support. We help clients manage their conditions at home through monitoring, medication support, education, lifestyle guidance, and close coordination with their physicians. The goal is simple: fewer complications, fewer emergency visits, and a higher quality of daily life.

Respite Care

Family caregivers give so much — and they need support too. Our respite care provides short-term, dependable coverage so you can rest, attend to your own health, work, or travel without worry. Whether you need a few hours, a full day, or longer-term coverage, we step in with the same trained, compassionate care your loved one receives every day.

Post-Surgery Care

The days and weeks after surgery are critical to recovery. Our team supports clients with wound monitoring, medication assistance, mobility and transfer help, pain-management support, and watchfulness for complications — all coordinated with your surgical team. We help make the transition from hospital to home smoother, safer, and less stressful for the whole family.

Catheter Care

Proper catheter care is essential to prevent infection and keep clients comfortable. Our nurses provide skilled catheter care and maintenance, and through nurse delegation, trained aides can safely support certain catheter-related tasks under RN supervision — allowing clients with ongoing needs to remain at home with confidence.

Enteral Feeds on G-Tube

For clients who receive nutrition through a gastrostomy (G-tube), we provide skilled oversight of enteral feeding, including formula administration, tube site care, and monitoring for complications. Where appropriate, these tasks may be delegated to trained aides under the supervision of a registered nurse delegator, ensuring safe, consistent nutrition support at home.

Supported Living Services

Supported living is about helping individuals — including those with disabilities or complex needs — live as independently as possible in their own home or community. Our support is flexible and person-centered, covering daily living skills, household tasks, community access, health and safety monitoring, and the coordination needed to keep life running smoothly. We tailor the level of assistance to each person's abilities and goals, always prioritizing autonomy and dignity.

Care Coordination

Good care depends on good communication. Our care coordination keeps every member of the care circle aligned — physicians, specialists, therapists, pharmacies, case managers, and family. We help schedule and prepare for appointments, share relevant updates across the team, manage transitions of care, and make sure nothing falls through the cracks. The result is care that feels seamless rather than fragmented.

Understanding Home Care

What Is Home Care?

Home care refers to the support that helps a person carry out the everyday activities of living — safely and comfortably — in their own home. It ranges from companionship and light assistance to hands-on personal care and skilled clinical services. Below are common areas where our team helps.

Respectful, hands-on assistance with bathing, showering, grooming, oral care, and incontinence care that preserves dignity.

Help with walking, repositioning, getting in and out of bed or chairs, and fall prevention to keep clients safe and active.

Planning and preparing nourishing meals tailored to dietary needs and preferences, plus feeding assistance when needed.

Timely reminders and organization to keep medications on schedule, with administration assistance where appropriate.

Conversation, activities, and a friendly presence that combats isolation and supports emotional wellbeing.
